Nassau focus

Royal Beach Club advantage

  • Opened December 2025: this is the newest Nassau-specific Royal Caribbean product on your route.
  • Included with pass: complimentary boat transfer, Wi-Fi, loungers, umbrellas, towels, lockers, showers, and all-day dining.
  • Three neighborhoods: Party Cove, Paradise Beach, and Chill Beach, each anchored by a pool and its own swim-up or nearby bars.
  • Scale: 3 pools, 2 beaches, 10 bars, 3 dining spots, and about 30 cabanas.
If you skip the club, Nassau still accepts U.S. dollars broadly and many local restaurants add a 15% service charge automatically.

Dining radar

Included vs premium meals

Included anchors

  • Windjammer: brunches, carving stations, and international theme nights.
  • Main Dining Room: rotating nightly menus; the app is the only reliable last-mile menu source.
  • Sorrento’s + Cafe Promenade: dependable late-night and between-show fuel.
  • El Loco Fresh, Park Cafe, Dog House, Sprinkles: casual speed plays on a short itinerary.

Premium targets

  • Royal Railway: five-course Wild West dinner; recent reviews call out short ribs and the gold bar dessert.
  • Mason Jar: Southern comfort standout; brunch is one of the ship’s biggest repeat recommendations.
  • Hooked: oysters, crab cakes, lobster rolls, raw bar style hits.
  • Giovanni’s, Chops, Izumi: safest specialty picks if you want classic Royal favorites.
If you only book one extra meal on this sailing, the highest chatter belongs to Royal Railway for novelty and Mason Jar for brunch comfort. Everything else is preference-driven.
Menu intelligence

What the menus really mean

  • MDR menus rotate and can shift by itinerary: do not lock your dinner plan until the Royal app shows the live nightly menu.
  • Specialty menus are more stable: Chops, Giovanni’s, Hooked, and Izumi tend to be consistent enough to reserve ahead.
  • Windjammer is stronger than many first-timers expect: Royal markets over 200 dishes across its nighttime buffet experience.
  • Short-sailing rule: a good included breakfast plus one deliberately chosen premium meal usually beats overbooking packages.
